Events

The Blindfold

A skit that breaks the myth around magic and it's wonders and pulls the curtain to show the science behind it. Unravelling the mystics and knowing what goes behind the veils of ignorance in the name of magic, when it's the science that plays the role.2:00PM-2:45PM

The Foundation Day Science Quiz

An exciting and fun set of science oriented questions for high school students! Teams of 3 or less. No cross college / school teams allowed. No problems will be asked, promise!2:00PM-3:30PM

Robotics Demonstrations

Robotics is a fascinating field among techies. We can see robots everywhere from factories to homes. Smartphone controlled robots, RF ID applications, Sensor based automation and much more will be demonstrated.
9:30AM-1:00PM

Chemistry Demonstrations

Ever wondered how it feels when your textbook knowledge of science physically manifests into a reality? Ever experienced those theories, those laws and principles coming to life, right before your eyes?
This is exactly what we plan to try and demonstrate through our exhibits, showcasing well known concepts from the world of chemistry through simple laboratory experiments; from colour-switching reactions to ‘cool’ liquid nitrogen tricks, from thermochromism to awesome batteries and much more!
9:30AM-1:00PM

Learning Science through Archaeology

The Palaeo-Arch lab will display stone tools made by prehistoric human ancestors, rock art and associated fossils of various animals along with proto and historical period artefacts. These specimens will be supplemented by informative posters on related subjects.
9:30AM-1:00PM

EES Demonstrations

1. Waste to Electricity and Winogradsky column - Utilization of electricity produced by growing microbes in a microbial fuel cell by utilizing wastewater/waste compounds.
2. Identification of rocks and minerals based on their physical and chemical parameters.
3. Comprehensive study of fossils to deduce evolutionary history of past life.
9:30AM-1:00PM

Mathematics+CS

It has been a long time since we have moved ahead from using a straightedge and a pair of compasses on a flat piece of paper.
With the invention of the "magical" machine that we call computers, Mathematics and Computer Science have worked hand in hand to take each other to greater heights. We shall demonstrate the beauty and power of these two subjects, which help in setting up the toolbox for various other disciplines.
From generating the fractals to the estimation of π, we hope that the demonstrations will grab your interest and motivate you to explore these fields in greater depth.
9:30AM-1:00PM

The Demonstrations of Life

Biology is a subject that encompasses everything around us. From the basic DNA molecules to the variations in the cognition of different sensory inputs by the brain; from analyzing the structure of microorganisms, to carrying out large scale population field-studies for endangered species; from the manipulation of the genetic makeup to study mutations, to the extraction of naturally occurring and artificial fluorescing compounds and their utilization in the laboratory
For this year’s Foundation Day, the biology demonstrations and exhibits would cover the above topics and many such interesting areas of biology.
9:30AM-1:00PM

Physics Demonstrations

Want to watch things defying gravity? Or machines working without any fuel? Or wanna watch things levitating? How about mixing and unmixing water colors? Sounds interesting? Here is your chance to see these exciting experiments and many more awaiting. Not only this, you will also get to interact with scholars and researchers, and visit their research labs where actual research experiments are carried out!
9:30AM-1:00PM

Literature

Treasure Hunt

This event is meant for students of 9-12 standard. This will be a mental and physical workout for students with the thrill of finding the treasure before anybody else. In the hunt you have to solve location puzzles and find the clue to the next location. Different teams will start hunt from different locations of the IISER Mohali campus and finally meeting at one point. We make sure you have fun and learn something new from here. Come in teams of 4 students from any class between 9 to 12.
Shoes are mandatory as clues may involve bushy areas.
Happy Treasure Hunt To You!
